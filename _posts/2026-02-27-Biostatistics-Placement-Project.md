This is a capstone project report, completed during my postgraduate studies in biostatistics. 

This project determined that the intensive care unit (ICU) bed that a patient stayed in, would not increase patient risk of developing delirium through known environmental risk factors for delirium in an ICU.

It achieved this by accounting for bed-level and patient-level factors in generalised linear models, generating outcome statistics akin to standardised mortality ratios (SMRs) and comparing these to a null model. 
After accounting for patient-level characteristics, it is shown that delirium incidence is not dependent on the particular bed that a patient occupied in this ICU via the effects of bed-level factors known to increase risk of delirium.  

That is, there was no risk increase due to bed assignment for developing delirium in this ICU.
