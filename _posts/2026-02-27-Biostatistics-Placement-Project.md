This is a capstone project report, completed during my postgraduate studies in biostatistics. 

This project determined that the intensive care unit (ICU) bed that a patient stayed in, would not increase patient risk of developing delirium through environmental risk factors for delirium in an ICU.

It achieved this using bed-level and patient-level parameterisation in generalised linear models, to compare statistics akin to standardised mortality ratios (SMRs) to a null model - showing no difference in delirium incidence due to the modelled risk factors.
